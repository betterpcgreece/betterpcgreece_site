The story

Why betterPC exists

🗄️

The problem with old hardware

Greece has millions of PCs from the 2006–2015 era sitting in storage. LGA775 machines, FX boxes, Core 2 Quads — most people assume they're useless. They're not. A €30–80 refresh (SSD + RAM) can give them another 3–5 years of life. betterPC helps you figure out what's worth fixing and what isn't.

🔧

The build configurator

No other Greek PC guide covers DDR2 and DDR3 platforms with realistic used-market pricing. The configurator on this site lets you price out a build — new or second-hand — in minutes, with real estimates and compatibility warnings. No clickbait, no affiliate links.

🇬🇷

Made for the Greek market

Prices include 24% Greek VAT and reflect what you'd actually pay locally or on platforms like Skroutz, e-shop, or second-hand Facebook/Marketplace groups. No US-centric Amazon prices that don't apply here.

📚

Plain language, real knowledge

No jargon without explanation. Every guide on this site is written so a first-timer can follow it, but goes deep enough that a veteran still learns something. That's the standard every page is held to.

Principles

What betterPC stands for

01
Honest pricing

Prices are estimates, labelled as such. No inflated numbers to make builds look cheap by comparison.

02
No ads, no affiliates

betterPC earns nothing from recommending specific brands. Picks are based on value and availability.

03
Old hardware matters

DDR2 and DDR3 machines can still serve real purposes. Helping people get more life from them is worth it.

04
Compatibility first

Nothing is recommended without a check. CPU–mobo–RAM compatibility is flagged before you waste money.

05
Greek context

Prices, availability, and part advice are grounded in the Greek and broader EU market, not the US.

06
Always improving

Parts lists, prices, and guides are updated as the market changes. This isn't a set-and-forget site.
